Master's Thesis

An Abstraction Layer for Multi-Repositories: Investigating the Virtual Monorepo Approach

University of Amsterdam · July 2025

Grade: 8.5

Driven by a passion for improving Developer Experience (DevEx), my master's thesis delves into the prevalent challenges of multi-repository systems. It explores the trade-offs between multi-repo and monorepo architectures, addressing common developer pain points like fragmented workflows and high cognitive load.

The research proposes a "virtual monorepo" as a pragmatic solution, offering the unified workflow of a monorepo without the disruptive cost of a full migration. The core contribution is a conceptual framework defining the key characteristics of this approach. To validate this, I designed and implemented a proof-of-concept tool that acts as an abstraction layer, demonstrating a significant reduction in workflow complexity and overhead. The project confirms that a virtual monorepo is a valuable strategy for enhancing developer productivity in distributed codebases.
